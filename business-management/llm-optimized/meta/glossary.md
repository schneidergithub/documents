## Market
A set of actual or potential customers, for a given set of products or services, who have common needs or wants, and can be reached through affordable means.  
Source: Session 3 (Sizing the Market). (S3 p29)

## Market Segment
A subset of a market that responds differently to some elements of the offering and references each other when making buying decisions.  
Source: Session 3 (Sizing the Market). (S3 p29)

## Strategy
Guides what goals to set and how to achieve them; informs and limits product decisions; aligns product strategy to market realities; reduces likelihood/magnitude of failure by identifying/managing risks.  
Source: Session 2 (Why is this needed?). (S2 p19)

## Technology Adoption Life Cycle (TALC)
A model of customer groups over time: innovators → early adopters → (chasm) → early majority → late majority → laggards.  
Source: Session 1/2 (TALC slide). (S1 p29, S2 p16)

## Product Life Cycle (PLC)
Stages over time: introduction → growth → maturity → decline, with different marketing/pricing/distribution strategies per stage.  
Source: Session 2 (PLC stages & strategies). (S2 p30–31)

## Value Discipline
Three differentiation archetypes: operational excellence, customer intimacy, product leadership.  
Source: Session 2 (Treacy & Wiersema). (S2 p33)

## Opportunity Analysis
A structured process that links objectives and strategy frameworks to: problems, segments, competitors, value prop, positioning, requirements, and sizing; plus business case/risk/budget for next stage.  
Source: Session 3 (Opportunity Analysis Process + Framework). (S3 p11–12)
