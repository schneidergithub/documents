## Research decision constraint
Always answer: **What decision am I going to make as a result of this research?** (S3 p25)

## Research types + example data sources (course list)

### Secondary
- Qualitative: websites, chat groups, publications, literature, in-house meetings.
- Quantitative: government statistics, analyst reports. (S3 p26)

### Primary
- Qualitative: industry experts, interviews, focus groups, usability studies, direct observation.
- Quantitative: internal business data (data mining), surveys, direct observation, experimental designs. (S3 p26)

## Getting close to customers (what to learn)

### Users care about
- Environment, process, outcomes, difficulties, and the required incremental improvement to change behavior. (S3 p27)

### Buyers care about
- Cost vs benefit, and required incremental improvement to impact business/situation. (S3 p27)

### Interview prompt bank (course list)
- How are you currently dealing with this task/problem?
- How does this task fit into the rest of the job?
- Who else is involved with this solution/process?
- What do you like about the current solution/process?
- What do you wish you could do that currently isn’t possible or practical?
- If you could do that, how would that make life better?  
Source: Session 3 (Getting Close to Customers: Ways…). (S3 p28)

## Segmentation dimensions (course list)
People segments:
- Psychographic (interests, behaviors, attitude)
- Geographic
- Demographic (income, age, education, gender)
- Job title
- Budgetary authority
- Family role (S3 p30)

Business segments:
- Geographic
- Industry (SIC code)
- Function (operations, purchasing, training, HR)
- Ownership (public, private, government)
- Company size (S3 p30)

## Caution
Geo/demographic similarity can hide meaningful differences (illustrated with example personas). (S3 p31)
