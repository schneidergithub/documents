## What is covered in these sessions

### Session 1 — Program context + estimation “Math Lab”
- Product management process topics covered in the course: target segment, problem definition, competitive positioning, release theme/driver, business value. (S1 p9)
- Estimation practice (“Math Lab”) method for sizing questions. (S1 p32–33)
- Technology Adoption Life Cycle (TALC) introduced (Moore). (S1 p29)

### Session 2 — Strategy frameworks
- Why strategy matters for product decisions (business context, objectives, market realities, risk). (S2 p19)
- Product Life Cycle (PLC) and stage strategies (Kotler). (S2 p30–31)
- Value disciplines (Treacy & Wiersema). (S2 p33)
- Competitive roles (Kotler). (S2 p34)
- Portfolio planning tools: BCG, Ansoff. (S2 p35–37)
- Strategy in context: vision → corporate → divisional → product/market → product objectives → marketing tactics. (S2 p40)

### Session 3 — Market opportunity analysis + research + problem statements
- Opportunity analysis process and required outputs. (S3 p11–12)
- Problem statement templates and rules (problem-only; buyer vs user vs influencer). (S3 p15, p19)
- Research types and sources; interview prompts. (S3 p25–28)
- Market and segment definitions; segmentation dimensions. (S3 p29–30)

## How to navigate this pack
- Start with `course/processes/opportunity_analysis.md` and `course/templates/problem_statements.md`.
- Use `course/frameworks/*` as “tools to think with”.
- Use `modern_addendum/alignment_overview.md` to map course outputs to contemporary product operating models.
